What is a USDA Guaranteed Loan?

The USDA Section 502 Guaranteed Loan Program helps moderate-income families purchase homes in eligible rural and suburban areas.

Key Benefits

  • 0% down payment required - No cash needed for down payment
  • Competitive interest rates - Often lower than conventional loans
  • Flexible credit requirements - More lenient than traditional mortgages
  • Lower mortgage insurance - 0.35% annual fee vs PMI at 0.5-1%
Did You Know? Many suburban areas qualify as "rural" for USDA purposes. Don't assume your area doesn't qualify!

Eligibility Requirements

Income Limits

  • Must be at or below 115% of area median income
  • 1-4 person household: Typically $119,850 maximum
  • 5-8 person household: Typically $158,250 maximum
  • All adult household income is counted

Credit Requirements

  • Minimum credit score: 640+ (lender-dependent)
  • ~700 credit score will secure best rates
  • Must explain any derogatory credit items

Property Requirements

  • Must be in USDA-eligible area
  • Must be your primary residence
  • Cannot own other adequate housing

Pre-Approval Process

Timeline: 1-3 Days

Steps:

  1. Research USDA-Approved Lenders
    • Compare 3-5 lenders for rates and fees
    • Check lender reviews and experience
  2. Submit Application
    • Complete loan application
    • Lender runs credit check (~$30-50)
    • Provide all required documents
  3. Receive Pre-Approval Letter
    • States maximum loan amount
    • Valid for 60-90 days
    • Required to make offers

Required Documents for Pre-Approval

Income Documentation

  • Last 2 years W-2s
  • Last 2 years tax returns (all pages)
  • Last 30 days pay stubs
  • Proof of additional income

Asset Documentation

  • 2 months bank statements (all accounts)
  • Investment statements
  • Retirement account statements

Identification

  • Driver's license or state ID
  • Social Security card

Other

  • Employer contact information
  • 12 months rental history (if applicable)
  • Divorce decree (if applicable)
  • Gift letter (if using gift funds)

Letters of Explanation

Underwriters frequently request written explanations for various items:

Common Situations Requiring Letters:

  1. Large Deposits - Any deposit over $500
  2. Credit Derogatory Items - Late payments, collections, charge-offs

Inspection & Appraisal

Home Inspection

  • Timeline: Within 7-10 days of offer acceptance
  • Cost: $300-600 (paid to inspector)
  • Your choice - Pick your own inspector

After Inspection, You Can:

  1. Request Repairs - Focus on safety/functional issues
  2. Request Price Reduction - Or closing cost credit
  3. Accept As-Is - If satisfied with condition
  4. Walk Away - If major deal-breakers found

Home Appraisal

  • Timeline: 7-14 days after ordering
  • Cost: $400-700 (paid by you)
  • Ordered by lender - Independent third-party

Appraisal Outcomes

If Appraisal < Purchase Price

Example: Buying for $300,000, appraises at $290,000

Your Options:

  1. Negotiate Price Reduction
    • Seller reduces to appraisal value
    • Most common with motivated sellers
  2. Bring Cash to Cover Gap
    • You provide $10,000 additional cash
    • Not ideal but keeps deal together
  3. Split the Difference
    • Common compromise
  4. Walk Away
    • If appraisal contingency in contract

Underwriting & USDA Approval

Timeline: Weeks 2-4

Lender Underwriting (2-3 weeks)

Underwriter reviews:

  • All financial documents
  • Credit report and history
  • Employment verification
  • Asset verification
  • Appraisal report
  • Title search results
  • Insurance verification

USDA Review (7-14 business days)

  • Lender submits complete package to USDA
  • USDA uses Guaranteed Underwriting System (GUS)
  • Automated underwriting for qualifying applications
  • Typical approval: 2-5 business days

Closing Preparation

Timeline: Week 4-5

Final Steps Before Closing:

  1. Homeowners Insurance
    • Must be in place before closing
    • Shop around for best rates
    • First year paid at closing
  2. Closing Disclosure
    • Receive at least 3 days before closing (required by law)
    • Review all loan terms and closing costs
    • Compare to Loan Estimate
    • Shows exact "Cash to Close"
  3. Final Walkthrough
    • 24-48 hours before closing
    • Verify repairs completed
    • Check utilities functional

USDA-Specific Information

Annual Fee Details:

  • 0.35% annual fee - Paid monthly with mortgage
  • Much cheaper than PMI (typically 0.5-1%)
  • Cannot be removed by reaching 20% equity
  • Only eliminated by refinancing to conventional

Property Must Remain Primary Residence:

  • Cannot convert to rental property
  • Must notify USDA if selling
  • No prepayment penalties - can pay off anytime

Additional USDA Benefits:

  • Payment assistance if experiencing hardship
  • Moratorium relief options
  • Section 504 repair grants/loans (if 62+ years old)
  • Streamlined refinance program available
